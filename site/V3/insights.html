Dealing with Disruptive Innovation & Digital Disruption

2020 brought us a pandemic of unimaginable scale, that has forced the world to re-think what business-as-usual means.  As we carefully step into a brand new year, we are forced to adapt to the new norm, safe distancing and wearing masks with frequency, remote work, disrupted personal spaces and elevated concern for our safety and health.   

Before COVID-19 we already know the most pertinent risk was the arrival of a new competitor in the market, with a better, faster, cheaper product or service, and worst of all, with the ability to reach markets, more imaginatively and efficiently.  Traditional responses have been to improve or expand your range of products and services on offer, but is this enough to address the digital disruptions and innovations we face today?

*With Uber, Airbnb, eBay, Alibaba, Netflix, Facebook and so on, being the top and most common services people use today, a thought-provoking pattern is on the rise. What do these services have in common, of course, apart from the fact that they run on digital platforms, operate via Internet connectivity, are usually cheaper, more convenient and on-demand?  They carry no traditional inventory or assets.

The challenges are complex:
- How to adapt our tools, culture, and processes to succeed in this new era?
- How to quickly deliver innovative, imaginative and new solutions whilst leveraging modern cloud and AI-driven architectures?
- How to update and improve existing solutions for faster time to market?

Our ability to grow and succeed in the long term, is dependent on our ability to elicit the combined power of transformed internal cultures to nurture a learning enterprise, and exponential access to gain insights from data; a culture of learning that transcends skills and training, rather it is about how we can create and agile enterprise with an open culture.

Our people at Saberlit are positioned to help you address these disruptions and challenges with confidence, by embracing Agile delivery methodologies and a Learning culture for continuous improvement.
